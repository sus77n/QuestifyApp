## EX 1.1.6
**Type**: Multiple Choice  
**Question**: Which of the following is the contrapositive of the implication "If P, then Q"?  
**Options**:  
- A. If Q, then P  
- B. If not Q, then not P  
- C. If not P, then not Q  
- D. If P, then not Q  
**Solution**: B

---

## EX 1.1.7
**Type**: Multiple Choice  
**Question**: Which logical connective is represented by the symbol "¬"?  
**Options**:  
- A. Conjunction  
- B. Disjunction  
- C. Negation  
- D. Biconditional  
**Solution**: C

---

## EX 1.1.8
**Type**: Multiple Choice  
**Question**: What is the truth value of the statement (P ∨ Q) if P is false and Q is true?  
**Options**:  
- A. True  
- B. False  
- C. Cannot be determined  
- D. Both true and false  
**Solution**: A

---

## EX 1.1.9
**Type**: Multiple Choice  
**Question**: Which of the following is logically equivalent to ¬(P ∧ Q)?  
**Options**:  
- A. (¬P ∧ Q)  
- B. (¬P ∨ ¬Q)  
- C. (P ∨ Q)  
- D. (P ∧ ¬Q)  
**Solution**: B

---

## EX 1.1.10
**Type**: Multiple Choice  
**Question**: Which of the following statements is always true?  
**Options**:  
- A. P ∧ ¬P  
- B. P ∨ ¬P  
- C. P → P  
- D. ¬(P ∨ Q)  
**Solution**: B

## EX 1.1.11
**Type**: Short Answer  
**Question**: Write the converse of the implication: "If it rains, then the ground is wet."  
**Solution**: If the ground is wet, then it rains.

---

## EX 1.1.12
**Type**: Short Answer  
**Question**: Construct the truth table for the expression P ↔ Q.  
**Solution**: 
P | Q | P ↔ Q
--|---|------
T | T |   T
T | F |   F
F | T |   F
F | F |   T

---

## EX 1.1.13
**Type**: Short Answer  
**Question**: What is the negation of the statement: "P or Q"?  
**Solution**: ¬P ∧ ¬Q

---

## EX 1.1.14
**Type**: Short Answer  
**Question**: Are the statements "P → Q" and "¬Q → ¬P" logically equivalent? Briefly explain.  
**Solution**: Yes, because "¬Q → ¬P" is the contrapositive of "P → Q", and a statement is logically equivalent to its contrapositive.

## EX 1.1.15
**Type**: Short Answer  
**Question**: Give an example of a propositional statement and state its possible truth values.  
**Solution**: Example: "The sky is blue." Its possible truth values are true or false.
