# Lesson 1.2: Propositional Equivalences

---

## EX 1.2.1  
**Type**: Multiple Choice  
**Question**: Which of the following expressions is logically equivalent to the expression (P ∧ Q)→(P ∨ Q)?  
**Options**:  
- A. (P ∧ Q) ∨ (P ∨ Q)  
- B. (P ∧ Q) ∨ ¬ (P ∨ Q)  
- C. ¬(P ∨ Q) ∨ (P ∨ Q)  
- D. (¬P ∨ ¬Q) ∨ (P ∨ Q)  
**Solution**: D

---

## EX 1.2.2  
**Type**: Multiple Choice  
**Question**: Which of the following laws is equivalent to P→Q?  
**Options**:  
- A. ¬P ∧ Q  
- B. ¬P ∨ Q  
- C. P ∨ ¬Q  
- D. P ∧ ¬Q  
**Solution**: B

---

## EX 1.2.3  
**Type**: Multiple Choice  
**Question**: Logical expression P ∨ Q ∨ [(¬P ∧ Q) ∨ ¬Q] is logically equivalent to what below expression?  
**Options**:  
- A. P ∨ Q  
- B. ¬P ∧ Q  
- C. ¬P ∧ ¬Q  
- D. ¬P ∨ Q  
**Solution**: A

---

## EX 1.2.4  
**Type**: Short Answer  
**Question**: Given P, Q, R are propositions.  
P: Triangle ABC is isosceles.  
Q: Triangle ABC is equilateral.  
R: Triangle ABC has three equal angles.  
The statement "If ABC is an equilateral triangle, then three angles are equal and it is an isosceles triangle" corresponds to which logical statement?  
**Solution**: P → (R ∧ Q)

---

## EX 1.2.5  
**Type**: Short Answer  
**Question**: Logical expression (¬P → R) ∧ (Q → R) is logically equivalent to what below expression?  
**Solution**: (P → Q) → R
